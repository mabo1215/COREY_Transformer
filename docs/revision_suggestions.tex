\section*{Summary of Recommendation}
\textbf{Borderline Reject (4/10).} The paper proposes an interesting
mechanism (entropy-guided chunk selection + Hadamard reparameterization)
but the empirical evidence does not support the claims implied by the
title. The central theorem is empirically falsified on real checkpoints
\emph{by the authors themselves}, the headline $3.24\times$ speedup
cannot be attributed to the entropy mechanism, and the prototype
ablations are derived from a deterministic cost model whose outputs
co-vary with the scheduling signal it evaluates.

\section{Major Issues}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C1] Theorem~1 is falsified on every tested real checkpoint.}
Section~4.1 reports that on \texttt{mamba-370m} layers 0--7, entropy
\emph{decreases} in $160/160$ (layer, sample) pairs (mean
$-1.40\pm0.37$ nats), with Sinkhorn $\ell_1$ residual
$1.700\pm0.029$, far above the $0.10$ threshold satisfied by the
synthetic regime. This means the central mechanism advertised in the
title (``entropy-guided'') does \emph{not} apply to the real models the
paper claims to target. Please either (a) reposition the paper as a
synthetic-regime study, or (b) provide a class of real activations
(e.g., post-Hadamard projections, intermediate scan states) where the
doubly-stochastic mixing condition empirically holds.
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C2] Table~2's $3.24\times$ speedup cannot be causally
attributed to entropy guidance.} The three policies differ
\emph{only} in chunk size ($1$, $64$, $256$). A static policy with
\texttt{chunk=256} would yield essentially the same speedup. The
required ablation is missing:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item A \emph{static-256} baseline (fixed chunk = 256, no entropy
        signal). If this matches COREY's latency, the entropy signal
        contributes nothing.
  \item A perturbation experiment where the entropy of the input is
        artificially varied and the chunk recommendation is shown to
        track it in a way that improves latency over a fixed-chunk
        oracle.
  \item Sweep \texttt{chunk} $\in \{32, 64, 128, 256, 512\}$ statically
        and report the latency curve. If entropy-selected chunk falls
        on the curve, no value is added beyond chunk-size tuning.
\end{enumerate}
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C3] All Tier-1 ablations are derived from a deterministic
Python cost model whose design encodes the conclusion.} The cost model
is a closed-form function of operator chain and activation statistics
(Appendix~A.5). Ablations over $(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)$, $\tau$, and
tiling policy in Tables~5, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12 therefore measure how the
chosen cost function rewards its own inputs, not how a real GPU
responds. Please either:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Validate the cost model against \emph{at least one} real-kernel
        latency series (e.g., Nsight Compute traces) showing
        $|\hat{t}_{\text{model}} - t_{\text{GPU}}| / t_{\text{GPU}} <
        0.2$ across the range used in ablations; \emph{or}
  \item Demote all Tier-1 tables to the appendix and label them
        ``illustrative cost-model behavior'' rather than ``ablation
        studies.''
\end{itemize}
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C4] The diagnostic proxy is circular and should be removed
from the evidence chain.} The authors acknowledge in Section~8 and
Table~8's caption that the proxy is constructed from the same entropy
and outlier signals that drive scheduling. Tables~8 and~11 should
either be removed or replaced with measured perplexity under W8A8/W4A8
on a real quantized Mamba checkpoint (e.g., via Quamba, which the
authors report installing successfully).
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C5] No checkpoint-level quality evidence is provided for
COREY itself.} Table~13 shows that \texttt{policy\_off} and
\texttt{policy\_corey} produce \emph{identical} NLP scores because the
hook is passive. The repeated claim ``no quality regression'' is
therefore tautological --- it follows from the implementation, not
from an experiment. Either:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Implement a version of COREY that actually modifies the
        forward computation (e.g., fused Hadamard absorbed into
        \texttt{in\_proj}) and report perplexity / LongBench against
        \texttt{policy\_off}; or
  \item Remove all ``no quality regression'' framing throughout the
        paper, since it conveys no information.
\end{itemize}
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C6] Anomalous WT103 PPL for Mamba-2.8B (\texttt{policy\_corey}
$=954.81$ vs.\ \texttt{policy\_off} $=329.80$).} If COREY is truly a
passive monitor, identical weights and identical FP16 path must yield
identical perplexity. A $2.9\times$ jump indicates either (a) the hook
is \emph{not} passive in the 2.8B configuration --- contradicting
Section~5.2 --- or (b) the harness has a bug. Please diagnose and
either remove the row or rerun with $n\geq 20$ matched to
\texttt{policy\_off}.
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C7] The ``fused COREY kernel'' does not exist in this
submission.} The Tier-2 speedups come from invoking the official
\texttt{mamba\_ssm.selective\_scan\_fn} with a different chunk size.
Algorithm~3 (Triton Fused SSM Kernel) is described prospectively but
never implemented or measured. The Abstract, Introduction, and
Section~3.3 should be revised to reflect that the contribution is a
\emph{scheduler over} an existing kernel, not a new fused kernel. As
written, a reader will reasonably infer that COREY produces a new
Triton kernel.
\end{majorbox}

\begin{majorbox}
\textbf{[C8] External baseline coverage is insufficient.} Pythia-410M
is the sole external comparison and the authors note it is
``architectural sanity-check'' rather than a fair systems baseline.
For a NeurIPS systems paper, please add at minimum:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Mamba-2 (Gu \& Dao, 2024) or RWKV-6 at comparable parameter
        counts;
  \item FlashAttention-3 + a comparably sized Transformer at the same
        sequence lengths (the workload COREY claims to address);
  \item A static-tuned chunk-size oracle on the same kernel (see C2).
\end{itemize}
\end{majorbox}

\section{Minor Issues}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M1]} Table~3 reports $n{=}1$, $0$ warmup, and negative latency
deltas the authors themselves call ``within measurement noise.'' Move
to appendix; do not present in the main results section.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M2]} The term ``Entropy-Guided Fusion'' overstates the
mechanism. No operator graph is fused by COREY; only chunk size is
selected. Suggest renaming throughout to ``Entropy-Guided Chunk
Selection'' or ``Entropy-Adaptive Tiling'' to avoid misleading
expectations.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M3]} Algorithm~1 references a generic score $S$ but Eq.~(4)
defines $S(R) = \alpha H_e(R) + \beta\,\mathrm{AI}_f(R) - \gamma
M_f(R)$. Algorithm~1 should explicitly carry $(\alpha, \beta, \gamma,
\Omega)$ as inputs.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M4]} Theorem~3's $\ell_\infty$ bound degrades to
$O(\sqrt{d})$ slack when $D=\Theta(d)$ outliers are present
(Appendix~C.3). This caveat should appear in the main text where the
theorem is stated, not only in the appendix.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M5]} The Pythia citation contains author-list errors
(e.g.,\ ``Kyle Khan, Arush Purohit'' do not appear on the actual
paper). Please verify against the published reference.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M6]} Figure~1's seven sequence-length series produce a
visually crowded plot. Consider a grouped bar chart with paired
pre/post-Hadamard bars, or a single delta-entropy curve, to make the
``post is always above pre'' claim immediately legible.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M7]} Section~5.2 states the checkpoint hook uses
$\tau_0 = 5.0$ nats, exceeding the theoretical maximum
$\log K \approx 4.16$. While the authors explain this is intentional
(passive monitor), a less confusing design is to set
$\tau_0 = +\infty$ explicitly and document the hook as
non-gating. The current value invites the reader to suspect a unit
error.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M8]} Tables~2 and 15 both report kernel-level results but use
different setups (different sequence configs and reported counters).
Consolidate into a single table with consistent columns.
\end{minorbox}

\begin{minorbox}
\textbf{[M9]} ``Speedup vs.\ \texttt{policy\_off}: $48.6\times$'' in
Table~15 is misleading because \texttt{policy\_off} is a Python
per-timestep loop --- it measures Python dispatch overhead, not a fair
unfused GPU baseline. Replace with comparison against the smallest
practical chunk (e.g., chunk = 1 implemented as a single Triton call
per timestep).
\end{minorbox}

\section{Suggested Revisions for Camera-Ready (if accepted)}

\begin{suggestbox}
\textbf{Revise the Abstract} to scope claims accurately. Suggested
rewrite of lines 9--12:
\begin{quote}\itshape
``We present COREY, a prototype \emph{scheduler} that selects
selective-scan chunk sizes from a runtime entropy estimate and absorbs
normalized Hadamard transforms into linear projections. COREY does not
introduce a new fused kernel; it dispatches the existing
\texttt{mamba\_ssm} Triton kernel with adaptive chunk sizes.''
\end{quote}
\end{suggestbox}

\begin{suggestbox}
\textbf{Restate Theorem~1's scope} in the main text. After the
theorem statement, add:
\begin{quote}\itshape
``We empirically verify that this condition fails for in-projection
outputs of pretrained Mamba-370M ($160/160$ pairs show entropy
\emph{decrease}). Theorem~1 should therefore be read as a
characterization of the synthetic heavy-tailed regime used for
prototype calibration, not as a property that transfers to deployed
checkpoints.''
\end{quote}
This is partly present in Remark~1 but should be elevated to the
theorem statement itself.
\end{suggestbox}

\begin{suggestbox}
\textbf{Add the missing static-256 baseline} to Table~2:
\begin{verbatim}
Static-256  256   16   ?.??   ?.??   ???.?x   ?.??x
\end{verbatim}
This single row will determine whether the paper's central empirical
result is attributable to entropy guidance or to chunk-size choice
alone.
\end{suggestbox}

\begin{suggestbox}
\textbf{Reorganize the Results section} as follows:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{6.1 Real-GPU kernel scheduling (Tier 2).} Lead with
        Table~2 \emph{plus} the static-256 ablation.
  \item \textbf{6.2 Online hook overhead (Tier 2).} Move Table~3 here
        with $n\geq 30$ measurements; if not feasible, move to
        appendix.
  \item \textbf{6.3 Cost-model behavior (Tier 1).} Clearly labelled as
        non-hardware. All current Tier-1 content goes here.
\end{enumerate}
\end{suggestbox}

\section*{Recommendation Rationale}

The paper has a kernel of a good idea: making chunk size respond to
input statistics. The Tier-2 evidence in Table~2 is the most
interesting result, but its causal attribution to entropy is not
established. The Tier-1 cost-model results, which constitute the
bulk of the empirical content, are not admissible evidence for a
systems claim because the cost function and the scheduler share
inputs. Combined with the falsification of Theorem~1 on real
checkpoints, the absence of any forward-modifying COREY variant, and
the unexplained PPL anomaly for Mamba-2.8B, the submission is not yet
ready for NeurIPS in its current form. With a static-chunk-size
ablation, a real-kernel validation of the cost model, and a
forward-modifying COREY variant with measured perplexity, this could
become a solid contribution.
