\section*{Summary}
%==============================================================================

COREY is a runtime chunk-size scheduler for the Mamba-1.x
\texttt{selective\_scan\_fn} Triton kernel. The scheduler computes a
Shannon entropy estimate over the post-convolution hidden state and maps it
to a chunk size via a log-scale rule. Evidence is organized in three tiers:
(Tier~1) a deterministic Python cost model; (Tier~2a) an active inline hook
on \texttt{MambaMixer.cuda\_kernels\_forward} measuring $8.3\%$ overhead;
(Tier~2b) a direct Triton scan-kernel timing showing $3.24\times$ (legacy
$H_\text{ref}{=}8.0$) and $4.41\times$ (calibrated $H_\text{ref}{=}\log K$)
speedup over static chunk-64. The authors explicitly flag that Tiers~2a and
2b are disconnected experiments and that the end-to-end integrated speedup
is unmeasured. Theorem~2 (entropy increase under Hadamard) is reported as
empirically falsified on 160/160 real Mamba-370M activation pairs.

Since Cycle~2 the paper has undergone substantial revision: inline informal
theorem statements, a full falsification paragraph in \S4, a principled
calibration ($H_\text{ref}{=}\log K$) as the default, tightened Limitations,
and two new scaffold sections (\S6.5 Integrated Measurement, \S6.6
Heterogeneous Workload). These additions show genuine responsiveness to prior
review.

\section*{Overall Assessment}
%==============================================================================

\begin{description}[leftmargin=2em,style=nextline]
  \item[Soundness:] 3 (good) --- claims are carefully scoped; the falsification
  of Theorem~2 is reported honestly. The Tier-2a/2b disconnect is now
  acknowledged in both Limitations and the dedicated sections.
  \item[Presentation:] 3 (good) --- the three-tier taxonomy is clear; the
  Signal~A / Signal~B separation is maintained consistently.
  \item[Contribution:] 2 (fair) --- the core system contribution remains a
  single-parameter auto-tuner with unmeasured end-to-end speedup.
  \item[Rating:] \textbf{4: Borderline Reject.}
  The paper is one successfully executed experiment away from borderline
  accept (Cycle~2 score was 5). The integrated measurement (§6.5) and the
  heterogeneous-corpus evaluation (§6.6) are both scaffolded but fully
  unexecuted: all table cells carry \texttt{[TODO]} placeholders. Submitting
  with two completely empty result sections is not appropriate for camera-ready
  or for a main-track NeurIPS submission. The score is lowered from 5 to 4
  accordingly.
  \item[Confidence:] 5 (very confident).
\end{description}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em]

  \item \textbf{Theorem-2 falsification in main text.} Moving the

\section{Major Weaknesses}
\label{sec:weaknesses}
%==============================================================================

\subsection{W1 (critical) --- §6.5 and §6.6 are empty scaffolds.}
The paper now contains two dedicated result sections that carry zero measured
data. §6.5 (\emph{End-to-End Integrated Measurement}) has three rows in
\texttt{tab:integrated}; the third row (Active + routed) has
\texttt{[TODO: measured value pending]} in every cell. §6.6
(\emph{Heterogeneous Real-Workload Evaluation}) has 15 \texttt{[TODO]} cells
across three entropy regimes.

A main-track systems paper cannot have result sections with no results. Either:
(a) execute the experiments before submission, or
(b) collapse §6.5 and §6.6 back into the Limitations as ``planned future
work'' and stop claiming in Limitations bullet~7 that the gap is ``targeted
for camera-ready'' (since it is currently not closed even for this draft).

The authors must choose one path and be consistent.

\subsection{W2 (critical) --- Table 1 COREY (default) RTX~3070 row is missing its measured latency.}
The COREY (default, $H_\text{ref}{=}\log K$) row in Table~1 at RTX~3070
shows \texttt{[TODO: measured calibrated latency pending rerun]}. The
speedup entries read ``$4.41\times$ (analytic)'' rather than a measured
number. This means the paper's headline result (the oracle-matching calibrated
setting) has never been directly timed end-to-end.

The RTX~3090 calibrated row is also empty. This is a 10-minute GPU job.
It should be executed before submission.

\subsection{W3 (high) --- §6.5/§6.6 prose uses present/past tense for unexecuted work.}
§6.5 reads: ``\textit{To close this gap, we modify
\texttt{MambaMixer.cuda\_kernels\_forward} so that...}''
§6.6 reads: ``\textit{All 60 prompts are run on Mamba-370M under
Active+routed integrated scheduling...}''

These statements are factually false if the experiments have not been run.
The present-tense description of experimental design that has not been
executed will mislead reviewers. The tense must be future/conditional
(``we will modify...'', ``will be run'') or the text recast explicitly as a
protocol description.

\subsection{W4 (high) --- Limitations bullet~7 contradiction.}
Limitations bullet~7 reads: ``\textit{End-to-end integration gap (targeted
for camera-ready).}'' This is the correct framing, but the same bullet says
``The integrated measurement protocol in \S6.5 is scaffolded and targeted for
camera-ready; \textbf{until the experiment is executed, the end-to-end speedup
of routing entropy-selected chunks into the live scan path is unmeasured.}''

The contradiction is: \S6.5 describes the experiment in present tense as if
it has been done, while Limitations bullet~7 explicitly admits it has not.
Readers who see the table \texttt{[TODO]} will understand, but the prose
discrepancy is a red flag for reviewers who read the two sections out of order.

\subsection{W5 (medium) --- Conclusion paragraph tension with §6.5 status.}
The Conclusion says: ``\textit{Routing the selected chunk into the scan
kernel---which would realize the $3.24\times$ speedup demonstrated in
Tier-2b---remains the next engineering step.}'' This is accurate but is now
in direct conflict with the title of §6.5, which claims this gap is being
addressed in this very paper. A reader who reads the Conclusion before
§6.5 will be confused.

\section{Minor Weaknesses}
%==============================================================================

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em,label=m\arabic*.]
  \item \textbf{§6.3 passive hook latency footnote.}
  Line~281 says the passive hook results use $n{=}1$, zero warmup. The active
  integration uses $n{=}5$, 2 warmup. This asymmetry is noted in the appendix
  but should also appear in the main-text footnote at first mention.
  \item \textbf{Scheduler configuration heterogeneity.}
  The prototype uses $K{=}64$ bins, but the checkpoint hook uses $K{=}256$.
  The two configurations are documented but only in separate subsections;
  a one-sentence ``these two $K$ values are for different evaluation paths''
  note in the Experimental Setup would prevent reader confusion.
  \item \textbf{Algorithm box and real kernel.}
  Algorithm~1 (\emph{Entropy-Guided Scheduling Boundary Selection}) operates
  on an ``operator chain $\mathcal{C}$'' with a generic scoring function.
  Readers unfamiliar with the paper history may not realize this algorithm
  is the Tier-1 prototype logic and not the Tier-2b Triton kernel invocation.
  Adding a one-sentence clarification (``this algorithm describes the
  Tier-1 prototype scheduling logic'') would prevent misreading.
  \item \textbf{Stale build note in main.tex.}
  A comment at line 395 reads ``Build note: paper/build/main.pdf compiled to
  31 pages on 2026-04-17.'' After the addition of §6.5 and §6.6, the page
  count will differ. Remove or update this comment.
  \item \textbf{MF-EN column.}
  Table~22 in the appendix still contains MF-EN exact-match scores (all
  zeros). The caption acknowledges this is a harness artifact. Replacing
  with token-level F1 or removing the column is cleaner.
  \item \textbf{Terminology alignment: §6.2 calibration paragraph.}
  Line~267 says ``\textit{The calibrated RTX~3070 latency is
  \texttt{[TODO]}...ms from a dedicated 30-repeat timing at chunk$=512$.
  The RTX~3090 calibrated run is pending.}'' Once the measurements are filled
  in, this paragraph should be rewritten in declarative form; the
  ``pending'' language must be removed.
  \item \textbf{`tab:active\_integration` reference in Limitations.}
  Limitations bullet~9 references \texttt{tab:active\_integration}. Verify
  that this label is defined in the appendix (it should be in
  \texttt{sec:active\_overhead}).
  \item \textbf{`tab:entropy\_variance\_real` vs `fig:entropy\_variance\_real`.}
  §6.4 references \texttt{Appendix Table~\ref{tab:entropy\_variance\_real}}
  (line~219) while §6.6 also references the same label. The appendix has
  \texttt{fig:entropy\_variance\_real} (a figure). Confirm whether this is
  a table or a figure and ensure the label type is consistent.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Questions for the Authors}
%==============================================================================

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em,label=Q\arabic*.]
  \item What prevented executing the integrated measurement (§6.5) and the
  heterogeneous corpus (§6.6) before this submission? Is there a technical
  obstacle (not just time) that warrants retaining the scaffold sections
  rather than collapsing them?
  \item For the calibrated latency of COREY (default, chunk$=512$, RTX~3070):
  Static-512 measured $0.748\pm0.037$\,ms with 30 repeats. Is there any
  reason to expect the calibrated COREY run to differ from Static-512, given
  that COREY (calibrated) selects the same chunk size? If not, $0.748$\,ms
  is already an empirical upper bound.
  \item The active-mode hook applies a $K{=}256$ histogram entropy estimate.
  The Tier-2b benchmark uses \texttt{torch.randn()} standard-normal activations.
  For the intended integrated experiment (§6.5), will the hook entropy
  measurement be performed on the same \texttt{torch.randn()} tensor as used
  in the Tier-2b benchmark, or on real Mamba-370M checkpoint activations during
  inference? The answer determines whether the 182-token prompt configuration or
  the synthetic 4096-token seq\_len configuration is the correct protocol.
  \item For the heterogeneous corpus (§6.6), the paper specifies ``20 prompts
  from HumanEval docstrings.'' HumanEval docstrings are typically very short
  (10--50 tokens). How will prompt length be standardized across the three
  entropy regimes to avoid confounding sequence-length effects with entropy
  effects?
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Final Recommendation}
%==============================================================================

\textbf{Score: 4 (Borderline Reject) $\to$ 5 (Borderline Accept) conditional
on R1--R3 being resolved before camera-ready.}

The authors have done everything asked in Cycle~2: the inline theorems,
the falsification paragraph, the calibrated default, the compressed prototype
tables, and the tightened limitations are all well-executed. The paper's
honesty and self-awareness remain unusual and commendable.

The only thing standing between this paper and a 5 is that the two most
important experiments (\S6.5 integrated measurement and \S6.6 heterogeneous
corpus) remain unexecuted, and the headline Table~1 result (calibrated
COREY latency) is still a \texttt{[TODO]}. These are each at most a few
hours of GPU work on the same RTX~3070 already used for other experiments.
Executing them would transform the paper from a well-described plan into a
genuinely complete Concept \& Feasibility contribution.

Without R1--R3, I recommend rejection for the current submission window and
resubmission once the experiments are complete.
